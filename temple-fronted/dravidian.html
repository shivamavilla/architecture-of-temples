Dravidian Style of Temple Architecture

üõï Introduction

Emerging in Tamil Nadu under the Pallava dynasty, Dravida temples are characterized by their tiered pyramid-like towers called Vimanas. These temples are enclosed within high compound walls and have towering Gopurams (gateway towers) richly decorated with stucco figures. The Garbhagriha is square and oriented along the cardinal directions. Temple complexes often feature temple tanks for ritual purification and various Mandapas supported by intricately carved pillars. The use of granite and soapstone is prevalent, and sculptures display deities, dancers, and divine guardians with ornate detailing. Dravida temples exemplify architectural symmetry and grandeur.

Basic of dravidian style

  • Origin: Southern India, under the Pallavas
  • Timeline: From the 6th century CE onwards
  • Place of Origin: Tamil Nadu
  • Main Features:
    • Vimana tower shaped like a stepped pyramid
    • Massive Gopurams (gateway towers) and multiple enclosures
    • Square Garbhagriha aligned with cardinal directions
    • Mandapas supported by intricately carved pillars
    • Temple tanks or water bodies within compound walls
  • Materials Used: Granite and soapstone; stucco on gopurams
  • Decorative Elements: Sculptures of gods, guardians, and mythical creatures with detailed iconography
