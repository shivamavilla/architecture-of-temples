Nagara Style of Temple Architecture

üõï Introduction

Originating during the Gupta period in Northern India, Nagara temples are known for their vertical emphasis and towering shikharas. Their sanctums are usually square-shaped and rest on elevated platforms. The Shikhara is curvilinear, resembling a beehive, and topped with a circular Amalaka and Kalasha finial. The temples often include multiple Mandapas, and their outer walls are adorned with intricate carvings. Temples under this style do not include large boundary walls or grand gateways like southern temples. Materials such as sandstone and granite are frequently used. The Nagara style flourished in regions like Madhya Pradesh, Odisha, and Rajasthan.

    • Base or Adhisthana decorated with ornamental friezes
    • Amalaka and Kalasha crowning the Shikhara
    • No boundary walls or large gopurams like in southern temples
  • Materials Used: Primarily sandstone and granite
  • Decorative Elements: Intricate sculptures depicting mythological tales, deities, and celestial beings
